# 2024 Global Experts Chart Course for International Biomaterial Cooperation at Taipei Tech Forum

Taipei Tech's High-Value Biomaterials Research and Commercialization Center (HBRCC, or MT3 +) held an international forum during the 2024 Bio Asia-Taiwan conference. Many renowned biomaterial experts from across the globe attended. Chaired by MT3 + director Fang Hsu-wei, attending experts discussed international collaboration models in biomaterials, sharing experiences in industry-academia cooperation to strengthen cross-border exchanges and advance research.

## Key Agreements
- Taipei Tech and the Institute of Biomedical Engineering and Nanomedicine (IBEN) at Taiwan's National Health Research Institute (NHRI) signed an agreement to promote academic and talent exchanges and advance technology transfer.
- Fang Hsu-wei emphasized the importance of commercializing research findings, citing successes such as patents on an artificial tear solution in Japan and Taiwan.

## Challenges and Opportunities
- Commercialization in biomedicine faces challenges due to regulatory, safety, and efficacy considerations. However, the agreement aims to create new opportunities in Taiwan's biomaterials field.
- Liao Lun-de, IBEN deputy director, highlighted that the NHRI is Taiwan's leading medical and biomedical research institute, and the collaboration will enhance technical research and talent cultivation.

## Collaboration Models
- The 3P (Public-Private-Partnerships) model was discussed as a potential game changer for academia-industry collaborations, with hopes of driving momentum in Taiwan's biomaterials industry.
- Professors from Japan, Israel, New Zealand, and Taiwan proposed various international collaboration opportunities, including:
  - Joint research papers and patent development between Israel and Taiwan.
  - Establishing cross-border educational programs.
  - Participating in global research networks.

## Strategic Recommendations
- Japan-Taiwan collaboration could build on existing semiconductor partnerships and expand into biomedical applications through nanotechnology.
- Experts recommended leveraging Taiwan's semiconductor expertise while strengthening biomanufacturing capabilities to compete regionally.
- The focus should shift from small-scale projects to larger international collaborations in innovative technologies and drug development.

## Conclusion
Fang stated that Taipei Tech, with strong research capabilities and alumni support, is well-positioned to advance research commercialization. Improvements in resource integration and market insights are needed to accelerate development and build new Taiwanese brands. He looks forward to forming closer international collaborations to open new horizons in the global biomedical industries.
