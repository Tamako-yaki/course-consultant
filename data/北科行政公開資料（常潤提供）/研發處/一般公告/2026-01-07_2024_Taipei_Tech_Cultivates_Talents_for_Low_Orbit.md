# 2024 Taipei Tech Cultivates Talents for Low Orbit Satellite Communications

Promoting space industry-academia cooperation! Taipei Tech is implementing the Ministry of Education's subsidized "B5G Low-orbit Satellite Critical Communication Module Industry Talent and Technology Cultivation Base" program. Recently, Taipei Tech has held a briefing session on the cooperation between enterprises and partner schools and a workshop for seed teachers, with the hope of building a bridge for industry-academia communication and cultivating future seed teachers to nurture talents in the integration of technologies such as radio frequency modules, communication and access networks, and system applications, so as to collaborate in enhancing Taiwan's international competitiveness in the field of satellite communications.

## Key Highlights
- **Program Overview**: Taipei Tech introduces the latest space technology and industry needs to students, enhancing their professionalism and preparing them for future employment.
- **Industry Participation**: The morning briefing was attended by representatives from several B5G satellite communication companies and organizations, including the Taiwan Space Agency (TASA), Pegatron, and the Chinese Taipei Amateur Radio League (CTARL).
- **Expert Presentations**: Mr. Wei-Chun Chen from TASA presented on the current development and future outlook of Taiwan's space industry.
- **Real-World Application**: Yuh-Shyan Hwang emphasized the importance of students engaging with real industry cases to apply their learning effectively.
- **Professional Training**: The afternoon workshop focused on B5G low-orbit satellite communication technology, featuring lectures aimed at enhancing teachers' theoretical knowledge and practical abilities.
- **Future Commitment**: Mr. Hsin-Piao Lin highlighted Taipei Tech's ongoing efforts to promote university-enterprise cooperation and improve the professionalism of both teachers and students in the field of satellite communication technology.
